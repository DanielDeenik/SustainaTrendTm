The Green Premium: How Sustainability is Reshaping Real Estate Value

Our analysis of 1,240 residential properties and 340 commercial buildings reveals a clear pattern: sustainability is no longer just a compliance requirementâ€”it's becoming a significant driver of real estate value across the Netherlands.

The data shows properties with top-tier energy performance (EPC labels A and B) command a 4.2% price premium in the sales market and up to a 5.8% premium in rental values compared to otherwise equivalent properties with lower energy ratings.

This "green premium" is accelerating. Our trend analysis indicates it has increased by approximately 1.2 percentage points annually over the past three years, with the strongest growth observed in urban areas where sustainability awareness is highest.

Green Financing: The Catalyst

Perhaps most notably, financial institutions are increasingly serving as catalysts for this transition. The share of green mortgages has risen by 18% in the last quarter alone, with preferential rates for energy-efficient properties creating a compelling financial incentive for buyers.

For housing corporations managing large portfolios, our data reveals that strategic investments in energy efficiency upgrades are yielding an average ROI of 7.2% when accounting for both operational savings and asset value appreciation.
